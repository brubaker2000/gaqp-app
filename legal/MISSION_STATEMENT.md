# Mission Statement
## Institute for Qualitative Standards (IQS)

---

The Institute for Qualitative Standards (IQS) is an independent, nonprofit standards organization whose mission is to develop, publish, maintain, and advance Generally Accepted Qualitative Principles (GAQP) — the open framework governing the extraction, classification, provenance, confidence, and reconstruction of qualitative intelligence.

IQS exists to establish the trust infrastructure for qualitative judgment: to give organizations the tools to make qualitative reasoning auditable, composable, and durable across time, sources, and the individuals who hold it — in the same way that Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP) established the trust infrastructure for financial reporting.

The Institute pursues this mission through four functions:

1. **Standards Development** — Defining and maintaining the GAQP constitutional and statutory layers: the claim type register, admission tests, confidence ladder, canonical schema, and sidecar format that together govern how qualitative claims are extracted and recorded.

2. **Education** — Producing and disseminating reference materials, implementation guidance, and deconstruction resources that enable organizations and individuals to apply GAQP correctly and consistently.

3. **Conformance** — Establishing and administering the L1–L7 conformance ladder that provides a legible compliance vocabulary for organizations building, buying, and auditing GAQP-compliant systems.

4. **Stewardship** — Maintaining the GAQP Standards Package as a public, open, machine-readable artifact available without license, fee, or attribution requirement to any organization that chooses to implement it.

---

**The public benefit served by IQS is the creation of a governed standard for qualitative intelligence** — a class of institutional knowledge that has remained ungoverned, undocumented, and unrepeatable for the entirety of the modern organization's existence. Every organization that makes decisions — which is every organization — operates within GAQP's potential jurisdiction. The standard is non-proprietary by design. The Institute derives no competitive advantage from restricting access to it.
