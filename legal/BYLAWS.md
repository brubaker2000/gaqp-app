# Bylaws of the Institute for Qualitative Standards
### A Delaware Nonprofit Nonstock Corporation

---

> **Note to counsel:** Working draft for attorney review. These Bylaws are designed for a standards body modeled on the FASB/Financial Accounting Foundation governance structure — an independent standards authority with a public benefit mission, a separated standards-setting function, and an open-publication policy. Key design choices: (1) nonmember structure at formation, with a path to an affiliate/implementer program; (2) Standards Committee with constitutional-layer jurisdiction; (3) separation between constitutional and statutory governance; (4) open publication mandate.

---

## Article I — General

**Section 1.1 — Name**

The name of the corporation is the Institute for Qualitative Standards (the "Institute" or "IQS").

**Section 1.2 — Principal Office**

The principal office of the Institute shall be located in Washington, D.C. The Institute may establish additional offices at such locations as the Board of Directors may determine.

**Section 1.3 — Purpose**

The Institute is organized and operated exclusively for charitable, educational, and scientific purposes as set forth in its Certificate of Incorporation. The Institute's specific mission is to develop, publish, maintain, and advance Generally Accepted Qualitative Principles (GAQP) as an open standard for governed qualitative intelligence — and to educate organizations, developers, and the public on its application.

**Section 1.4 — Fiscal Year**

The fiscal year of the Institute shall begin on January 1 and end on December 31 of each year.

---

## Article II — Board of Directors

**Section 2.1 — Authority and Responsibility**

The Board of Directors (the "Board") is the governing body of the Institute. The Board is responsible for:

(a) Setting the strategic direction of the Institute;

(b) Ensuring the Institute operates in furtherance of its charitable and educational purposes;

(c) Overseeing the financial integrity of the Institute;

(d) Appointing and evaluating the Executive Director;

(e) Approving the annual budget; and

(f) Ensuring that the GAQP Standards Package is maintained, published, and freely available to the public.

**Section 2.2 — Composition**

The Board shall consist of not fewer than three (3) and not more than fifteen (15) directors. The exact number shall be fixed by resolution of the Board. A majority of directors must be independent — they must have no direct financial interest in any organization that derives revenue from GAQP-compliant software products or certification services.

**Section 2.3 — Terms**

Directors shall serve staggered three-year terms. Directors may serve a maximum of three consecutive full terms (nine years), after which they must rotate off the Board for at least one year before becoming eligible for re-election.

**Section 2.4 — Election**

Directors shall be elected by the affirmative vote of a majority of directors then in office, at any regular or special meeting of the Board. Initial directors are identified in the Certificate of Incorporation.

**Section 2.5 — Vacancies**

Any vacancy on the Board, including a vacancy resulting from an increase in the number of directors, shall be filled by a majority vote of the remaining directors. A director elected to fill a vacancy shall hold office for the remainder of the unexpired term.

**Section 2.6 — Removal**

A director may be removed, with or without cause, by a two-thirds vote of the directors then in office at any special meeting of the Board called for that purpose.

**Section 2.7 — Resignation**

A director may resign at any time by delivering written notice to the Chair or the Secretary. Resignation is effective upon receipt unless a later date is specified.

**Section 2.8 — Compensation**

Directors shall not receive compensation for their service as directors. The Board may authorize reimbursement of reasonable expenses incurred in the performance of Board duties.

---

## Article III — Meetings of the Board

**Section 3.1 — Regular Meetings**

The Board shall hold not fewer than two (2) regular meetings per year. The time and place of regular meetings shall be determined by the Chair or by resolution of the Board.

**Section 3.2 — Annual Meeting**

The Board shall hold an annual meeting each year for the purpose of reviewing the Institute's activities, approving the annual report, and conducting such other business as may properly come before the Board.

**Section 3.3 — Special Meetings**

Special meetings of the Board may be called by the Chair, the Executive Director, or any two directors, upon at least five (5) days written notice to all directors.

**Section 3.4 — Notice**

Notice of all meetings shall be given by written communication (including email) at least five (5) days before the meeting, except in cases of emergency where shorter notice may be given. Notice shall state the date, time, place, and general purpose of the meeting.

**Section 3.5 — Quorum**

A majority of the directors then in office constitutes a quorum for the transaction of business. If a quorum is not present, a majority of the directors present may adjourn the meeting.

**Section 3.6 — Action**

The act of a majority of the directors present at a meeting at which a quorum is present shall be the act of the Board, unless a greater number is required by law, by the Certificate of Incorporation, or by these Bylaws.

**Section 3.7 — Remote Participation**

Directors may participate in any meeting by means of telephone, video conference, or other communications equipment that allows all participants to hear each other simultaneously. Such participation constitutes presence at the meeting.

**Section 3.8 — Action Without Meeting**

Any action required or permitted to be taken at a meeting of the Board may be taken without a meeting if all directors consent in writing (including by email) to the action. Such consent shall have the same effect as a unanimous vote.

---

## Article IV — Officers

**Section 4.1 — Officers**

The officers of the Institute shall be a Chair, a Vice Chair, a Secretary, and a Treasurer. The Board may appoint such additional officers as it deems necessary. No person may simultaneously hold the offices of Chair and Secretary.

**Section 4.2 — Chair**

The Chair shall preside at all meetings of the Board, shall have general oversight of the affairs of the Institute, and shall perform such other duties as the Board may prescribe.

**Section 4.3 — Vice Chair**

The Vice Chair shall perform the duties of the Chair in the Chair's absence and shall perform such other duties as the Board may prescribe.

**Section 4.4 — Secretary**

The Secretary shall keep the minutes of all Board meetings, shall give notice of meetings as required by these Bylaws, shall maintain the records of the Institute, and shall perform such other duties as the Board may prescribe.

**Section 4.5 — Treasurer**

The Treasurer shall have oversight of the financial affairs of the Institute, shall ensure that accurate financial records are maintained, shall present financial reports to the Board, and shall perform such other duties as the Board may prescribe.

**Section 4.6 — Executive Director**

The Board may appoint an Executive Director to manage the day-to-day operations of the Institute. The Executive Director serves at the pleasure of the Board and reports directly to the Board. The Executive Director is not a director and does not vote on Board matters. The Executive Director's compensation shall be set by the Board and shall be reasonable and consistent with comparable positions at similar organizations.

**Section 4.7 — Election and Terms**

Officers shall be elected by the Board at the annual meeting and shall serve for one-year terms. Officers may be re-elected without term limit.

**Section 4.8 — Removal**

Any officer may be removed by a majority vote of the directors then in office whenever, in the Board's judgment, the best interests of the Institute would be served thereby.

## Article V — Committees

**Section 5.1 — Standards Committee**

The Board shall maintain a Standards Committee (the "Committee") that is responsible for the technical governance of the GAQP Standards Package. The Committee shall:

(a) Have sole jurisdiction over proposed changes to the GAQP Constitutional Layer, which includes the claim type register, admission tests, metadata schema, source anchor schema, .intel file format, conformance level definitions, and governing principles;

(b) Review and approve proposed additions to the GAQP Statutory Layer, which includes domain subtags, cross-cutting tag values, and sector crossref corrections;

(c) Maintain the GAQP Schematic as a versioned, machine-readable artifact and publish new versions upon approval;

(d) Establish and administer the conformance certification process; and

(e) Process community proposals for Statutory Layer additions within thirty (30) days of submission.

The Standards Committee shall consist of not fewer than three (3) members appointed by the Board. Members need not be directors. At least one member shall have domain expertise in the application of the standard to operational contexts.

**Section 5.2 — Constitutional vs. Statutory Jurisdiction**

The following distinction governs the Committee's authority:

*Constitutional Layer* — Changes to the claim type register, admission tests, schema fields, .intel format, conformance levels, and governing principles require formal Committee review, Board approval, and a version increment. No GAQP-compliant application may unilaterally alter constitutional elements.

*Statutory Layer* — New domain subtags within existing sectors, new cross-cutting tag values, and sector crossref corrections may be added by Committee resolution without Board approval or a version increment. The Committee shall publish accepted statutory additions in the next scheduled release of the Schematic.

**Section 5.3 — Finance and Audit Committee**

The Board shall maintain a Finance and Audit Committee responsible for oversight of the Institute's financial affairs, review of annual financial statements, selection of auditors, and review of the annual IRS Form 990.

**Section 5.4 — Additional Committees**

The Board may establish such additional committees as it deems necessary. No committee shall have authority to act on behalf of the Board with respect to: amending the Certificate of Incorporation or Bylaws; electing or removing directors or officers; approving mergers or dissolutions; or any matter reserved to the Board by law.

## Article VI — Standards Development Process

**Section 6.1 — Open Publication Mandate**

The GAQP Standards Package shall be published and maintained as a freely available public resource. Access to the current working version of the Schematic shall not require registration, payment, or membership. This obligation is a constitutional commitment of the Institute and may not be altered except by amendment to these Bylaws with a two-thirds vote of the Board.

**Section 6.2 — Versioning**

The Standards Package shall follow a versioning protocol in which:

(a) Working drafts carry a `-working` suffix;

(b) Frozen releases carry a release date, a SHA-256 package checksum, and a clean version designator;

(c) Minor version increments reflect constitutional layer changes;

(d) Statutory layer additions (new subtags, tag values) do not require a version increment but are documented in the release log.

**Section 6.3 — Community Proposals**

The Institute shall maintain a public proposal process for Statutory Layer additions. Any person or organization may submit a proposal through the Institute's public repository. The Standards Committee Chair, or any Committee member designated by the Chair, may approve Statutory Layer additions unilaterally and publish them immediately. There is no mandatory waiting period for Statutory Layer changes. The Committee shall review approved additions at its next regular meeting and may reverse an addition that does not meet the standards of the taxonomy; such reversals shall be documented in the release log.

The rationale for this design is explicit: GAQP's Statutory Layer consists of controlled vocabulary — subtags, tag values, and sector crossref corrections. Adding a new subtag harms no one. Delaying a necessary subtag harms the utility of the standard. Speed is a feature of a living vocabulary.

**Section 6.4 — Constitutional Change Process**

GAQP governs language synthesis and qualitative knowledge classification. It does not touch financial transactions, safety-critical systems, medical devices, or any other domain where a poorly designed change creates risk to persons or institutions. The constitutional change process reflects this risk profile.

Proposed changes to the Constitutional Layer require:

(a) A written proposal submitted to the Standards Committee describing the change and its rationale;

(b) Notice to registered implementers of not fewer than fourteen (14) days before the Standards Committee vote, so that implementers may prepare for the change;

(c) Approval by a majority vote of the Standards Committee; and

(d) Notification to the Board at its next regular meeting.

Board approval is not required for Constitutional Layer changes. The Standards Committee is the technical authority of the Institute. The Board's role is strategic and financial oversight, not technical gatekeeping. There is no mandatory public comment period. The fourteen-day implementer notice period exists to give compliant systems time to update, not to invite external governance of the Institute's technical decisions.

**Section 6.5 — Conformance Certification**

The Institute shall establish and administer a conformance certification program under which software systems and implementations may be evaluated against the L1–L7 conformance ladder defined in the GAQP Standards Package. Certification fees, if any, shall be reasonable and shall not create a barrier to entry for small implementers.

## Article VII — Financial Matters

**Section 7.1 — Contracts and Instruments**

The Board may authorize any officer, agent, or employee to enter into any contract or execute any instrument on behalf of the Institute. Such authority may be general or confined to specific instances.

**Section 7.2 — Checks and Drafts**

All checks, drafts, or other orders for payment of money issued in the name of the Institute shall be signed by such officers or agents as the Board may from time to time designate.

**Section 7.3 — Deposits**

All funds of the Institute shall be deposited to the credit of the Institute in such banks or depositories as the Board may designate.

**Section 7.4 — Annual Audit**

The Institute shall cause its financial statements to be reviewed or audited annually by an independent certified public accountant. The Finance and Audit Committee shall oversee the engagement.

**Section 7.5 — Conflicts of Interest**

The Institute shall adopt and maintain a Conflicts of Interest Policy consistent with IRS requirements for 501(c)(3) organizations. Any director, officer, or committee member with a material financial interest in a matter before the Board or a Committee shall disclose that interest and shall recuse themselves from deliberation and voting on that matter.

---

## Article VIII — Indemnification and Insurance

**Section 8.1 — Indemnification**

The Institute shall indemnify its current and former directors, officers, employees, and authorized agents to the fullest extent permitted by Delaware law against expenses, judgments, fines, and amounts paid in settlement actually and reasonably incurred in connection with any proceeding arising from their service to the Institute, provided that such person acted in good faith and in a manner they reasonably believed to be in or not opposed to the best interests of the Institute.

**Section 8.2 — Insurance**

The Board may authorize the purchase of directors' and officers' liability insurance and such other insurance as it deems appropriate for the protection of the Institute and its directors, officers, and employees.

---

## Article IX — Records and Reports

**Section 9.1 — Records**

The Institute shall keep adequate and correct books and records of account, minutes of the proceedings of the Board and all committees, and such other records as may be required by law.

**Section 9.2 — Annual Report**

The Institute shall prepare an annual report within 120 days after the close of each fiscal year. The annual report shall include: a description of the Institute's activities during the year; a summary of changes to the GAQP Standards Package; and a financial summary. The annual report shall be made publicly available on the Institute's website.

**Section 9.3 — Form 990**

The Institute shall file IRS Form 990 annually as required by law and shall make such filing publicly available.

---

## Article X — Amendments

**Section 10.1 — Amendment Process**

These Bylaws may be amended, repealed, or replaced by a two-thirds vote of the directors then in office at any meeting of the Board at which a quorum is present, provided that:

(a) Written notice of the proposed amendment, including the text of the proposed change, shall be given to all directors not fewer than fourteen (14) days before the meeting; and

(b) No amendment shall be made that would cause the Institute to fail to qualify as an exempt organization under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code.

**Section 10.2 — Open Publication Mandate**

Section 6.1 (Open Publication Mandate) may only be amended by a two-thirds vote of the directors then in office. Written notice of the proposed amendment shall be given to all directors not fewer than thirty (30) days before the vote. There is no mandatory public comment period. The Open Publication Mandate is a commitment to the implementer community and the public; the thirty-day director notice period ensures the Board acts deliberately on this commitment, not that external parties govern it.

---

## Article XI — Dissolution

Upon the dissolution of the Institute, after paying or adequately providing for all liabilities of the Institute, the remaining assets shall be transferred to one or more organizations organized and operated exclusively for charitable, educational, or scientific purposes that qualify as exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code, with preference given to organizations whose missions are related to standards development, open knowledge infrastructure, or qualitative intelligence governance. The Board shall determine the recipient organization(s).
