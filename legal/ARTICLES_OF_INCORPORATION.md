## Article III — Purposes

**Section 3.1 — General Purpose**

The corporation is organized exclusively for charitable, educational, and scientific purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (the "Code"), or the corresponding provision of any future federal tax law.

**Section 3.2 — Specific Purposes**

Without limiting the generality of Section 3.1, the specific purposes of the corporation are:

(a) To develop, publish, maintain, and advance Generally Accepted Qualitative Principles (GAQP), an open framework governing the extraction, classification, provenance, confidence scoring, and reconstruction of qualitative intelligence from documents, records, and other source materials;

(b) To establish, administer, and evolve a conformance framework against which software systems, organizations, and implementations may be evaluated for compliance with GAQP;

(c) To educate organizations, developers, researchers, and the general public on the principles, methods, and applications of governed qualitative reasoning;

(d) To produce, distribute, and maintain machine-readable standards artifacts — including the GAQP Schematic, sector vocabulary, domain subtag taxonomy, and canonical schema — as freely available public resources;

(e) To conduct and support research into the theory and practice of qualitative intelligence governance, institutional knowledge management, and the application of artificial intelligence to governed qualitative reasoning;

(f) To engage with government agencies, academic institutions, standards bodies, and private organizations to promote the adoption and proper implementation of GAQP; and

(g) To do all other acts and things incidental to, or in furtherance of, the foregoing purposes that are consistent with the corporation's status as a tax-exempt organization under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code.

---

## Article IV — Nonstock Corporation

The corporation is a nonstock corporation. It shall have no authority to issue capital stock. The corporation shall have no members within the meaning of Section 102(a)(4) of the Delaware General Corporation Law.

> **Note to counsel:** If IQS later establishes a formal membership program (e.g., for Certified Implementers), this Article should be amended to authorize a membership class. For initial formation, nonstock is appropriate.

---

## Article V — No Private Inurement

No part of the net earnings of the corporation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, its directors, officers, or other private persons, except that the corporation shall be authorized and empowered to pay reasonable compensation for services rendered and to make payments and distributions in furtherance of the purposes set forth in Article III.

---

## Article VI — Prohibited Activities

Notwithstanding any other provision of this Certificate, the corporation shall not carry on any activities not permitted to be carried on:

(a) By a corporation exempt from federal income tax under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code; or

(b) By a corporation, contributions to which are deductible under Section 170(c)(2) of the Code.

The corporation shall not devote a substantial part of its activities to carrying on propaganda, or otherwise attempting to influence legislation, and the corporation shall not participate in, or intervene in (including the publishing or distributing of statements), any political campaign on behalf of or in opposition to any candidate for public office.

## Article IX — Liability of Directors

To the fullest extent permitted by the Delaware General Corporation Law as it exists or as it may hereafter be amended, a director of the corporation shall not be personally liable to the corporation for monetary damages for breach of fiduciary duty as a director, except for liability:

(a) For any breach of the director's duty of loyalty to the corporation;

(b) For acts or omissions not in good faith or which involve intentional misconduct or a knowing violation of law;

(c) Under Section 174 of the Delaware General Corporation Law (relating to unlawful payment of dividends or unlawful stock purchase or redemption); or

(d) For any transaction from which the director derived an improper personal benefit.

---

## Article X — Indemnification

The corporation shall, to the fullest extent permitted by applicable law, indemnify its current and former directors, officers, employees, and agents against all expenses, judgments, fines, and amounts paid in settlement actually and reasonably incurred in connection with any threatened, pending, or completed action, suit, or proceeding arising out of their service to the corporation.

---

## Article XI — Dissolution

Upon the dissolution of the corporation, after paying or adequately providing for the debts and obligations of the corporation, the remaining assets shall be distributed to one or more organizations organized and operated exclusively for charitable, educational, or scientific purposes that shall at the time qualify as exempt organizations under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code, as the Board shall determine. No assets shall be distributed to any director, officer, member, or private individual.

---

## Article XII — Amendment

This Certificate of Incorporation may be amended in the manner provided by the Delaware General Corporation Law and the Bylaws of the corporation, provided that no amendment shall be made that would cause the corporation to fail to qualify as an exempt organization under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code.
