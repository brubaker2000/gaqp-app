Section 1.0

The Foundational Problem

GAQP was conceived to address a structural limitation in knowledge management: the document is the smallest indivisible unit of storage in every existing system.

The Document as Container

Every knowledge management system — filing cabinets, databases, content repositories, and modern AI retrieval systems — treats the document as the atomic unit of intelligence. A document can be stored, retrieved, searched, and summarized. The content inside it cannot be addressed independently of the whole.

This creates a structural constraint. A claim on page 4 of one document cannot be directly compared to a contradicting claim on page 11 of another without a human reading both. Intelligence cannot be filtered by the type of assertion it represents, the confidence level it has earned, or whether it was inferred or directly stated. The document boundary is the retrieval boundary.

The consequence is that qualitative knowledge inside organizations exists largely as dark matter. It is present, it influences decisions, but it cannot be systematically retrieved, compared, or governed. Post-mortems, field reports, expert assessments, and institutional lessons learned accumulate in document repositories where they remain effectively inaccessible to any governed reasoning process.

The GAQP Resolution

GAQP was designed to resolve this constraint by reducing the atomic unit of qualitative intelligence from the document to the individual governed claim — the nugget.

A nugget is an independently addressable qualitative assertion carrying its own type classification, source anchor, confidence level, inference flag, sector tags, and provenance chain. It does not require the document it came from to be retrieved, understood, or activated.

The reduction in atomic unit size from document to nugget produces an exponential increase in retrieval granularity. The document is a container. The nugget is an intelligence primitive.

Section 2.0

The GAQP Framework

GAQP defines the rules under which qualitative claims are extracted from source documents, classified, provisioned with provenance, assigned confidence, and made available for governed reconstruction. The framework comprises four layers: the admission layer, the classification layer, the confidence layer, and the reconstruction layer.

Section 2.1

Seven Admission Tests

Every candidate claim extracted from a source document must satisfy all seven admission tests before it enters a governed corpus. A claim that fails any test is not recorded. There is no partial admission.

Table 2.1 — Admission Tests
Test Name Pass Condition
01 Standalone Retains full meaning outside its source paragraph without surrounding context.
02 Disputability A reasonable and informed person can agree, challenge, or qualify the assertion.
03 Governance Can be tagged, sourced, scoped, and audited under the GAQP metadata schema.
04 Activation A governed system can determine when and where this claim is relevant to a decision.
05 Durability The claim retains usefulness beyond the moment and context of its original utterance.
06 Composability The claim can combine with other governed claims to produce larger judgment structures.
07 Non-Triviality The claim carries strategic, interpretive, factual, operational, or diagnostic weight.
Section 2.2

Twenty-Five Claim Types

Every admitted claim is assigned exactly one type from the following closed register. The claim type determines how the claim is weighted, routed, and composed during reconstruction.

Table 2.2 — Claim Type Register (partial)
Claim TypeOperational Definition
AxiomA foundational assumption treated as self-evident within a domain. Not subject to direct evidence but underpins other claims.
Causal ClaimAsserts that one condition produces, enables, or prevents another.
Diagnostic SignalAn observable indicator that something is wrong, emerging, or at risk. May not yet constitute a finding.
DoctrineA governing operating belief held by an institution, leadership, or system. Not necessarily evidence-based.
HeuristicA practical decision rule that works reliably enough to be applied without full analysis.
Institutional PrecedentA prior decision, ruling, or action that now constrains or shapes future choices.
ObservationA direct, evidenced assertion about what exists, occurred, or was measured.
TendencyA pattern that holds often but not always. Directional without being deterministic.
ThreatA condition, actor, or trajectory that poses risk to an objective, asset, or position.
OpportunityA condition that creates potential for value creation, advantage, or mission success.

Section 2.3

Confidence Ladder

Confidence is a governed property of each nugget. It advances through independent corroboration only. Repetition from the same source does not promote a claim. Structural elevation to the highest confidence level requires operator action and is never automated. A claim in active unresolved contradiction is frozen and cannot be promoted until the contradiction is resolved.

Section 2.4

Ten Governing Principles

The following principles constitute the constitutional layer of GAQP. They take precedence over implementation guidance where any conflict exists.

Table 2.4 — Governing Principles
#PrincipleRequirement
1Materiality of JudgmentPreserve claims that can influence a decision, interpretation, or risk posture. Discard what cannot.
2Source TraceabilityEvery admitted nugget must point back to its precise origin within the source document.
3Consistency of ApplicationApply the same rules uniformly across all claim processing, regardless of source or context.
4ConservatismUncertain qualitative claims must be weighted cautiously unless independently corroborated.
5Contextual Boundary DisclosureDisclose when claims are activated in a context different from their source context.
6Multi-Source CorroborationConfidence advances through independent convergence, not repetition. Same-source repetition is not corroboration.
7Judgment IndependenceSynthesis must not be contaminated by the interests of any single source or actor.
8ProportionalityThe weight of a claim should be proportional to the strength of its evidence.
9Revision StandardGAQP outputs must remain updateable when better information arrives.
10Scenario CompletenessSynthesis should account for alternative scenarios, not just the most likely.
Section 3.0

Tag Architecture

The GAQP tag system provides the coordinate structure that makes admitted nuggets independently queryable, composable across document boundaries, and routable to the correct function, risk owner, or decision context. Tags are not metadata decoration. They are the dimensional model that transforms a corpus of governed claims into a queryable intelligence system.

Every admitted nugget carries two distinct tag layers. The domain subtag layer places the claim precisely within a sector's operational vocabulary. The cross-cutting tag layer addresses dimensions common across all sectors — organizational function, risk category, subject topic, and geographic scope. The two layers operate independently and are combined in a single pipe-delimited field.

Section 3.1

Domain Subtags

Domain subtags are sector-specific. Each of the 31 GAQP sectors carries its own subtag taxonomy developed to the operational vocabulary of that industry. A claim tagged MIL:HUMINT is placed in the Military sector, Intelligence dimension, Human Intelligence subdomain. A claim tagged FIN:LBO is placed in the Financial sector, leveraged buyout subdomain.

Domain subtags answer the question: where does this claim live in the commercial and operational landscape? The current Standards Package contains 3,828 domain subtags across 31 sectors. Subtag additions within an existing sector do not require a version increment.

Section 3.2

Section 3.3

Combined Tag Structure

A nugget may carry multiple values within each prefix and values across all four prefixes simultaneously. The full tag string is pipe-delimited. The following example shows a single admitted claim from a HUMINT intelligence report on adversary supply chain activity in the Indo-Pacific theater.

Example — Fully Tagged Nugget (MIL sector · Intelligence dimension)
claim_type Diagnostic Signal
claim_text Adversary procurement activity in rare earth materials suggests
pre-positioning for sustained conflict operations.
inference_flag TRUE — claim extends beyond explicit source statement
confidence_level seed — single source, no independent corroboration yet
domain_tags_pipe MIL:HUMINT|MIL:THREAT_ASSESS|MIL:SOURCE_RELIA|MIL:INTEL_PROVNC
tags_pipe func:Intelligence|risk:StrategicSurprise|risk:SupplyChain_MIL
|topic:IntelProvenance|geo:IndoPacific

This single nugget is independently surfaceable from nine entry points — by claim type, by two domain dimensions, by two source reliability subtags, by organizational function, by two distinct risk categories, by topic, and by geography — without retrieving the document it came from.

Section 3.4

Retrieval Implications

The tag architecture transforms the retrieval problem. An undeconstructed document corpus can be searched by keyword or document title. A GAQP-governed corpus can be queried by any combination of claim type, confidence level, sector, domain subtag, function, risk category, topic, entity, and geography simultaneously.

Queries Enabled by Tag Architecture — Impossible Against Undeconstructed Corpus
Query 1 Every Diagnostic Signal tagged risk:VendorConcentration, sourced from operations documents, with confidence at developing or above, contradicted by any finance document in the corpus.
Query 2 Every Causal Claim tagged func:Intelligence and geo:IndoPacific, inference-flagged TRUE, across all source types in the corpus.
Query 3 Every claim tagged topic:LessonsLearned and MIL:AFTER_ACTION, ranked by confidence level, from the last 24 months of operational reports.
Query 4 Every Institutional Precedent tagged func:Legal and risk:Regulatory, regardless of sector, with a source anchor in a formal ruling or consent decree.
Query 5 Every claim tagged topic:Deterrence where confidence is disputed — active contradictions unresolved across source documents.

The same nugget corpus can be reconstructed through entirely different lenses by varying the tag filters applied at query time. The corpus does not change. The question changes.

Section 5.0

Conformance Levels

GAQP defines seven conformance levels representing increasing degrees of implementation maturity. A language model executing the Standards Package deconstruction prompt against a document corpus achieves L3 conformance without additional tooling. Higher conformance levels require governed infrastructure.

Level Name Capability
L1 Classification Claims classified using the 25-type GAQP register.
L2 Provenance Claims carry source anchors linking to precise locations in the originating document.
L3 Admissibility Claims pass all seven admission tests before entering the corpus. This is the baseline conformance level achieved by the standard LLM deconstruction workflow with no additional infrastructure.
L4 Confidence Claims carry governed confidence levels based on independent corroboration tracked across sources.
L5 Sidecar-Enabled Source documents carry .intel sidecar files — governed intelligence packages that travel with the source document and remain linked to it.
L6 Cross-Document Corpus Multiple .intel files are queryable as a unified governed corpus. Contradiction detection and corroboration mapping are operational across document boundaries.
L7 Activation Governed claims surface at the moment of decision in role-aware applications. The complete governed reasoning environment.
