Governing Statement

GAQP is not a feature of any application. Any application that implements it correctly is a GAQP-compliant platform. The standard is separable from any implementation. The implementation is not the standard.

Section 1.0

The Foundational Problem

For forty years, organizations have built systems to manage numbers. Spreadsheets. Financial models. Analytics platforms. ERP systems. The quantitative infrastructure of the modern organization is deep, auditable, and trusted.

They have never built a system to manage thinking.

Every acquisition, every market entry, every leadership hire — the numbers are downstream of qualitative judgment. The signal that a pattern is repeating lives in the head of whoever was in the room, until they leave. Qualitative intelligence has remained ungoverned, undocumented, and unrepeatable.

The Container Problem

In every existing system — document stores, knowledge bases, vector databases, semantic search — the document is the smallest indivisible container. A memo, a transcript, a board deck, a policy manual. The intelligence inside it is trapped in a bulky container.

This is like trying to run an economy where the smallest denomination is a $100 bill. You can transact, but poorly. You cannot price things precisely. You cannot combine three small insights from different documents into one new strategic conclusion. You cannot audit the exact logic that produced a judgment.

GAQP replaces the document with the atomic nugget as the unit of intelligence. A nugget is the smallest durable unit of qualitative reasoning that can stand alone outside its source, be classified by type, be traced to a source passage, be disputed or corroborated, and be composed with other nuggets into larger judgment structures.

The Compression Signal

Observation across knowledge domains suggests that a body of discourse compresses to atomic nuggets at approximately 44:1. A 32,000-word conversation yields approximately 25 governing atomic nuggets. Organizations are currently storing and acting on 44 times more noise than signal. GAQP isolates the signal.

The Result: Qualitative Liquidity

Breaking the document into governed atomic units produces qualitative liquidity — the ability to divide, compare, combine, audit, route, and reuse qualitative intelligence at any scale:

Divisibility

A document breaks into smaller, independently usable pieces of intelligence.

Comparability

One claim compares against another from a different source, time, or author.

Auditability

The system shows exactly where a conclusion came from — source, passage, timestamp.

Combinability

Nuggets from different documents recombine into new strategic conclusions.

Portability

A nugget travels from one context to another without dragging the whole document behind it.

Activation

The system reuses the nugget when a future situation matches its conditions.

The Three Functions

GAQP governs qualitative intelligence across three distinct functions. Each has a defined jurisdiction.

1 — Harvesting

The operator-driven process of identifying which documents and sources contain qualitative intelligence worth deconstructing. GAQP does not prescribe harvesting decisions. Operators choose what enters the pipeline.

2 — Deconstruction

The governed extraction of atomic nuggets from a source document. GAQP's core jurisdiction. Every candidate claim must pass all seven admission tests. Only admitted claims are recorded.

3 — Reconstruction

The application-driven synthesis of nuggets into conclusions, reports, recommendations, and actions. Reconstruction is outside GAQP's jurisdiction. GAQP ensures the ingredients are trustworthy. The recipes belong to the operator.

The Seven Admission Tests

Every candidate claim must pass all seven gates before admission. A claim that fails any test is not born. It is not recorded anywhere. Existence in a compliant system is proof of admission.

  • Standalone — The claim can be understood without its surrounding context.
  • Disputability — The claim can be meaningfully challenged or contradicted.
  • Governance — The claim has an identifiable source and author.
  • Activation — The claim would be useful to retrieve in a future decision context.
  • Durability — The claim is expected to remain valid beyond the immediate moment.
  • Composability — The claim can combine with other nuggets into larger conclusions.
  • Non-Triviality — The claim adds something not already known to the corpus.

Section 3.0

Tag Architecture

GAQP uses two tagging dimensions that operate independently and serve different purposes. Both are pipe-delimited fields on the nugget record.

3.1 Domain Subtags — domain_tags_pipe

Domain subtags classify the subject matter of a nugget within specific industry sectors. Every subtag is anchored to a sector code, making the sector implicit in the tag itself.

Format: SectorCode:SubtagCode

Field: domain_tags_pipe — pipe-delimited, multiple values permitted

domain_tags_pipe: "HC:MOB|HC:Medicare|RE:MOB"

Subtags are organized by dimension within each sector. The Military (MIL) sector, for example, has 15 dimensions including the unique GAQP Intelligence Functions dimension — which classifies the type of military document being deconstructed (OPORD, SITREP, AAR, etc.), not just its subject matter.

Statutory Layer — Extensible Without Version Bump

Domain subtags are part of the statutory layer. New subtags can be added to existing sectors via the IQS community suggestion process without requiring a version bump. The current release contains 3,827 subtags across 31 sectors.

3.2 Cross-Cutting Tags — tags_pipe

Cross-cutting tags classify dimensions that apply regardless of sector — organizational function, risk category, subject topic, and geography. These tags work with nuggets from any sector and enable retrieval across sector boundaries.

Format: prefix:Value

Field: tags_pipe — pipe-delimited, multiple values permitted

func:
Business Function — The organizational or operational function the claim pertains to. Examples: func:Strategy, func:CommandControl, func:Intelligence
risk:
Risk Category — The type of risk the claim describes or implies. Examples: risk:Geopolitical, risk:MissionFailure, risk:CyberCompromise
topic:
Subject Topic — A cross-sector subject the claim addresses. Examples: topic:Leadership, topic:Deterrence, topic:NationalSecurity
geo:
Geography — Geographic scope relevant to the claim. Examples: geo:IndoPacific, geo:NATO_Europe, geo:Taiwan_Strait

3.3 Combined Tag Structure

Both fields are used together on the same nugget record. Domain tags provide vertical specificity. Cross-cutting tags provide horizontal reach. A single nugget from a military intelligence briefing about Chinese naval activity in the South China Sea would carry:

domain_tags_pipe: "MIL:INTEL_ESTIMATE|MIL:ORDER_BATTLE|MIL:MARITIME_OPS" tags_pipe: "func:Intelligence|risk:StrategicSurprise|topic:NationalSecurity|geo:SouthChinaSea"

The two fields are additive, never redundant. domain_tags_pipe answers: what operational subject? tags_pipe answers: what function, what risk, what topic, what geography?

3.4 Retrieval Implications

The two-layer architecture enables retrieval strategies that neither layer supports alone:

  • Vertical drill-down — All nuggets tagged MIL:COIN across any source document, any time period, any author.
  • Cross-sector horizontal — All nuggets tagged risk:StrategicSurprise regardless of whether the source is military, geopolitical, or financial.
  • Intersection queries — All nuggets tagged both MIL:DETERRENCE and geo:Taiwan_Strait — sector-specific subject matter at a specific geography.
  • Confidence-filtered — Only nuggets at corroborated (0.91) or above, tagged topic:NationalSecurity.
  • Structural equivalence — Nuggets that reason identically despite using different surface language, revealed through matching claim type + tag fingerprint.

Semantic Similarity vs. Structural Equivalence

Vector databases answer: where have I seen this before? GAQP answers: what does this mean, and is it trustworthy? Two claims can sound alike and reason differently. GAQP's tag architecture finds structural equivalence — not surface similarity.

Section 4.0

Sector Vocabulary

GAQP's sector taxonomy is derived from operational demand structure and is the primary classification. GICS, SIC, and NAICS fields are cross-reference bridges for interoperability with financial and regulatory data systems — they do not govern GAQP classification.

Version 0.2 adds the Military (MIL) sector — 247 subtags across 15 dimensions. MIL is operationally focused: doctrine, intelligence, command, warfighting, readiness, and law of armed conflict. It is distinct from Aerospace & Defense (AD), which covers the contractor, platform, and industrial base side.

Section 5.0

Conformance Levels

GAQP defines a seven-level conformance ladder. Conformance levels create a legible compliance vocabulary for operators, buyers, and auditors evaluating compliant implementations.

Level Designation Minimum Requirement
L1 Schema Compliant All 29 required fields present on every nugget record.
L2 Type Classified L1 + every nugget mapped to one of the 25 canonical claim types.
L3 Admission Governed L2 + all seven admission tests applied during extraction. No rejected records stored.
L4 Source Anchored L3 + source anchor records with character offsets and quote hashes for every nugget.
L5 Confidence Tracked L4 + confidence ladder maintained, advancing only through independent corroboration.
L6 Sidecar Packaged L5 + nuggets delivered in .intel sidecar format with all 14 required sections.
L7 Corpus Governed L6 + cross-document corpus management, stale detection, and hash integrity verification.

Ten Core Principles

1
Materiality of JudgmentDistinguish decision-relevant qualitative factors from noise. Not all information is a claim.
2
Source TraceabilityEvery claim must be traceable to an identifiable source, actor, and passage. No floating assertions.
3
Consistency of Framework ApplicationThe classification framework applied to claims must remain constant across periods and contexts.
4
ConservatismWeight cautious interpretation. Optimistic readings must be explicitly justified.
5
Contextual Boundary DisclosureClaims must disclose the conditions under which they are valid and the conditions under which they break down.
6
Multi-Source CorroborationThree or more independent sources converging on a claim constitutes a finding. Same-source repetition does not.
7
Judgment IndependenceThe analyst or system producing a claim must be separated from distorting incentives.
8
ProportionalityThe depth of analysis applied to a claim should be proportional to the stakes of the decision it supports.
9
Revision StandardThe conditions under which a claim would require revision must be definable in advance.
10
Scenario CompletenessAnalysis must account for principal alternative interpretations, not only the preferred one.

Governance & Contribution

The Schematic has two tiers with different change friction:

  • Constitutional layer — Claim types, admission tests, schema fields, .intel format, conformance levels. These require a formal IQS review and version increment. No compliant application may alter these unilaterally.
  • Statutory layer — Domain subtags, cross-cutting tag values, sector crossref notes. These accept community contributions via the structured proposal process in CONTRIBUTING.md. IQS reviews proposals within 30 days.

To propose a new subtag or tag value, open an issue in the repository following the format described in CONTRIBUTING.md.
