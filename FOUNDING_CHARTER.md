# GAQP Founding Charter
## Generally Accepted Qualitative Principles
### Published by the Institute for Qualitative Standards (IQS)
### Version 1.0 — Working Draft · May 2026

---

> **Governing Statement:** GAQP is not a feature of any application. Any application that implements it correctly is a GAQP-compliant platform. The standard is separable from any implementation. The implementation is not the standard.

---

## Preamble

For forty years, organizations have built systems to manage numbers. Spreadsheets. Financial models. Analytics platforms. ERP systems. Data warehouses. The quantitative infrastructure of the modern organization is deep, auditable, and trusted.

They have never built a system to manage thinking.

Every acquisition, every market entry, every leadership hire — the numbers are downstream of qualitative judgment. Margins erode before the spreadsheet notices. Markets shift because an executive read the room correctly. Companies fail because nobody recorded what the last generation knew. The signal that a pattern is repeating — exactly as it did three years ago — lives in the head of whoever was in the room, until they leave.

Qualitative intelligence: strategy, competitive diagnosis, institutional precedent, risk assessment, expert judgment — has remained ungoverned, undocumented, and unrepeatable. It cannot be audited, compared across time, or activated by machines without losing its meaning.

**Generally Accepted Qualitative Principles (GAQP) is the answer to that gap.**

---

## I. The Historical Moment

The Large Language Model is the first tool in history capable of processing and synthesizing qualitative information at computational speed. What the spreadsheet did for number-crunching, the LLM does for thought-crunching. This is not a marginal improvement. It is a categorical leap that opens a new territory the market has not yet named or governed.

But an ungoverned LLM is a liability dressed as an asset. It is a probabilistic output machine — optimized for fluency, prone to hallucination, incapable of enforcing consistent judgment across time. It predicts the next token. It does not govern decisions.

The AI market has responded with thousands of point-solution agents and narrow wrappers — systems that solve one workflow slice, ungoverned and unauditable. None have addressed the foundational question:

**Who defines the standard for qualitative judgment?**

Nobody. That is the blue ocean. GAQP claims that territory.

---

## II. What GAQP Is

GAQP is a standard — not a product, not a platform, not a feature. It defines the rules that govern what a valid qualitative claim is, how it is classified, how its confidence evolves, and how it is packaged for machine consumption. Any software system may be GAQP-compliant by implementing these rules correctly.

**The GAAP parallel is precise and intentional.**

GAAP governs how financial facts are recorded, classified, and reported. GAQP governs how qualitative facts are recorded, classified, and reported. GAAP created the trust infrastructure for capital markets. GAQP creates the trust infrastructure for qualitative intelligence.

Every financial outcome is downstream of a qualitative decision. That means GAQP's surface area is larger than GAAP's. Every organization that makes decisions — which is every organization — operates within GAQP's jurisdiction.

---

## III. The Atomic Nugget

GAQP's fundamental unit is the **atomic nugget**: the smallest durable unit of qualitative reasoning that can stand alone outside its source, be classified by type, be traced to a source passage, be disputed or corroborated, and be composed with other nuggets into larger judgment structures.

Not every sentence is a nugget. A nugget is a sentence that survives governance.

### The $100 Bill Problem

Before GAQP, the document is the smallest unit most systems handle — a memo, a transcript, a board deck, a policy manual. The intelligence inside it is trapped in a bulky container. This is like trying to run an economy where the smallest denomination is a $100 bill. You can transact, but poorly. You cannot price things precisely. You cannot combine three small insights from different documents into one new strategic conclusion. You cannot audit the exact logic that produced a judgment.

GAQP breaks the $100 bill into spendable denominations of qualitative value.

The result is **qualitative liquidity** — the ability to divide, compare, combine, audit, route, and reuse qualitative intelligence, just as a currency system allows commerce to happen at any scale, in any direction, with any participant:

- **Divisibility** — a document breaks into smaller usable pieces
- **Comparability** — one claim compares against another from a different source
- **Auditability** — the system shows where a conclusion came from
- **Combinability** — nuggets from different documents recombine into new strategic conclusions
- **Portability** — a nugget travels from one context to another without dragging the whole document behind it
- **Activation** — the system reuses the nugget when a future situation calls for it

### The Compression Signal

Observation across knowledge domains suggests that a body of discourse compresses to atomic nuggets at approximately 44:1. A 32,000-word conversation yields approximately 25 governing atomic nuggets. Organizations are currently storing and acting on 44 times more noise than signal. GAQP isolates the signal.

## IV. The Three Functions

GAQP governs qualitative intelligence across three distinct functions. Each has a defined jurisdiction.

**1. Harvesting** — The operator-driven process of identifying which documents and sources contain qualitative intelligence worth deconstructing. GAQP does not prescribe harvesting decisions. Operators choose what enters the pipeline.

**2. Deconstruction** — The governed extraction of atomic nuggets from a source document. This is GAQP's core jurisdiction. Every candidate claim must pass all seven admission tests. Only admitted claims are recorded. There are no rejected records in a compliant system — only admitted claims and silence. The deconstruction process is governed by the GAQP Schematic, which any compliant implementation downloads and executes against.

**3. Reconstruction** — The application-driven synthesis of nuggets into conclusions, reports, recommendations, and actions. Reconstruction is outside GAQP's jurisdiction. How a compliant application reassembles and activates nuggets is its own affair. GAQP ensures the ingredients are trustworthy. The recipes belong to the operator.

This separation is deliberate and important. GAQP governs the extraction layer. The synthesis layer belongs to the market.

## V. The Constitutional Architecture

GAQP's rules divide into two tiers with different friction levels. This is the same design principle that makes GAAP stable: core standards change rarely and deliberately; specific treatments are added regularly as new instruments emerge.

### The Constitutional Layer

Immutable within a major version. Changes require formal IQS review and a version increment. Every downstream implementation must update to remain compliant.

**The 25-Type Closed Claim Register** — Every admitted nugget maps to exactly one of 25 canonical claim types. The register is closed. Operators may not invent types. The list is exhaustive, covering the full range from foundational truths to diagnostic signals to evaluative conclusions: Axiom, Definition, Ontological Assertion, Principle, Doctrine, Heuristic, Best Practice, Tendency, Observation, Event, Institutional Precedent, Constraint, Threshold Condition, Objective, Tradeoff, Causal Claim, Declaration of Value, Diagnostic Signal, Stakeholder Complaint Claim, Strength, Weakness, Opportunity, Threat, Asset, Liability.

**The Seven Admission Tests** — Every candidate claim must pass all seven gates before admission: Standalone, Disputability, Governance, Activation, Durability, Composability, Non-Triviality. A claim that fails any test is not born. It is not recorded anywhere. Existence in a compliant system is proof of admission.

**The Six-Level Confidence Ladder** — seed (0.50) → single_source (0.65) → developing (0.72) → corroborated (0.91) → structural (1.00) → disputed (score frozen). Confidence advances only through independent corroboration. Same-source repetition does not promote. A new extraction always begins at seed.

**The 29-Field Canonical Schema** — Every admitted nugget carries 29 fields covering identity, content, source provenance, classification, confidence, lifecycle, validation, generation, and annotation. The schema ensures every nugget is self-contained and auditable without reference to its original source context.

**The .intel Sidecar Format** — A structured sidecar file named `{source_filename}.intel` that packages all admitted claims from a source document. The sidecar travels with the source file. It contains only source-local admitted claims. No cross-document content is permitted in a single .intel file.

### The Statutory Layer

Extensible without a version bump. IQS updates these regularly as industries evolve, new sectors emerge, and the taxonomy deepens. The statutory layer is where the schematic grows continuously.

- **Sector Vocabulary** — 30 canonical industry sectors with crosswalks to GICS, SIC, and NAICS for interoperability with external systems
- **Domain Subtag Taxonomy** — Currently 3,580 subtags across all 30 sectors, organized by dimension, used in the `domain_tags_pipe` field
- **Cross-Cutting Tags** — Four controlled prefixes for the `tags_pipe` field: `func:` (business function), `risk:` (risk category), `topic:` (subject matter), `geo:` (geography)

The constitutional layer is stable. The statutory layer compounds over time. This is by design.

## VI. The GAQP Schematic

The **Schematic** is IQS's machine-readable runtime artifact — a versioned, downloadable bundle of JSON files that any compliant implementation loads to govern its deconstruction process.

A compliant application does not hardcode GAQP's rules. It loads the Schematic and executes against it. When IQS publishes an update — new subtags, refined sector coverage, additional taxonomy — compliant applications pull the new version and benefit immediately. The Schematic is infrastructure. Compliant applications are the market that runs on it.

The current Schematic (`v0.1-working`) includes: the claim type register, admission test specifications, confidence ladder, canonical nugget schema, source anchor schema, .intel file format specification, sector vocabulary, domain subtag taxonomy, cross-cutting tag vocabulary, conformance levels, and core principles.

**The Schematic is the product. Compliant applications are the market.**

## VII. Governance

GAQP is published and maintained by the **Institute for Qualitative Standards (IQS)**.

IQS is a standards body, not a software vendor. Its function is to define, maintain, and evolve the GAQP standard. Implementations are built by the market. IQS governs the rules they must meet.

### Conformance Levels (L1–L7)

GAQP defines a seven-level conformance ladder. A system that classifies nuggets by type but does not maintain source anchors is L2-compliant. A system that implements full .intel sidecar packaging with anchor coverage, hash integrity, and cross-document corpus management reaches L7. Conformance levels create a legible compliance vocabulary for operators, buyers, and auditors evaluating compliant implementations.

### Change Governance

The constitutional layer requires formal IQS review and a version increment for any modification. The statutory layer accepts community contributions — new subtags, additional tag values, sector crossref corrections — via a structured proposal process documented in the repository. IQS reviews proposals within 30 days.

No GAQP-compliant application may unilaterally alter the constitutional enumerations. All constitutional changes originate at IQS and are adopted downstream by compliant implementations.

### Versioning Protocol

Working drafts carry the `-working` suffix. Frozen releases carry a release date, a SHA-256 package checksum, and a clean version designator. The checksum is computed at freeze across all Schematic files, providing tamper-detection for downloaded packages. Compliant applications declare which version of the Schematic they implement.

## VIII. The Market Disruption

The qualitative intelligence market is large, diffuse, and currently defended by incumbents whose business models depend on keeping it ungoverned.

**Management consulting** charges millions of dollars for qualitative analysis that is episodic, unrepeatable, and undocumented. The report expires. The analyst leaves. The judgment that cost $2 million in fees cannot be retrieved, audited, or reactivated two years later. GAQP converts that episodic judgment into governed infrastructure. Infrastructure compounds. Reports do not.

**Knowledge management platforms** store documents. They do not govern their intelligence. Search returns the document. It does not return the specific atomic claim inside the document that is relevant to today's decision. GAQP operates one layer above document storage: at the level of governed, classified, activatable intelligence extracted from those documents.

**Vector databases and semantic search** answer: *where have I seen this before?* GAQP answers: *what does this mean, and is it trustworthy?* Semantic similarity is not structural equivalence. Two claims can sound alike and reason differently. GAQP finds structural equivalence — not surface similarity.

**Ungoverned LLM deployments** are becoming a liability in regulated environments. Every auditor, regulator, and board asking "how did the system reach that conclusion?" exposes the ungoverned LLM's fundamental weakness: it cannot show its work because it has no governed work to show. GAQP is the answer to that question. Every output is traceable to admitted claims. Every admitted claim is traceable to a source passage. The chain of reasoning is auditable by design.

**The disruption is jurisdictional, not technical.** GAQP defines the territory and names the rules. Once the market begins asking "is this system GAQP-compliant?" — the category belongs to the institution that defined it.

The parallel is exact. GAAP did not merely help accountants. It transformed the trust infrastructure of capital markets. Compliance became mandatory. Audit became a profession. The standard became the moat. GAQP is positioned to do the same for the infrastructure of qualitative judgment.

## IX. The Economic Model

GAQP creates value simultaneously at the standards layer, the implementation layer, and the organizational layer.

### For IQS

**Schematic licensing** — The working-draft Schematic is open. Production-certified versions, extended domain modules, and verified conformance testing carry licensing structures. Organizations building commercial implementations on GAQP have strong incentives to certify.

**Compliance certification** — The "GAQP Certified" designation signals to buyers that an implementation has been independently verified against the Schematic. In regulated industries — financial services, healthcare, legal, government — buyers will require it.

**Premium domain taxonomy** — IQS maintains and extends the statutory layer. Deeper subtag taxonomies for specialized verticals represent a subscription channel. A healthcare system with 2,000 controlled clinical subtags has more value to a hospital operator than the general 84-subtag starter set.

**Professional services** — Organizations building GAQP-compliant systems require implementation guidance. IQS-affiliated consulting creates a professional services channel without competing with the implementation market itself.

### For Compliant Implementations

**First-mover position** — The first compliant implementation in any vertical captures the reference-implementation position. Reference implementations define the standard's practical interpretation. That position is structurally difficult to displace.

**Corpus compounding** — Every document deconstructed deepens the corpus. Every nugget admitted raises the intelligence density of the system. Unlike a consulting report, the corpus does not expire. An organization that begins building its governed corpus in 2026 has a structural advantage over one that begins in 2028. The gap widens with every document deconstructed.

**Confidence network effects** — As independent sources corroborate nuggets, confidence scores advance. An organization with a deep corpus across many independent sources produces corroborated-level intelligence (0.91) where a thin corpus produces only seed-level claims (0.50). The Confidence Ladder encodes this network effect into the standard.

**Audit defensibility** — In regulated environments, the ability to produce an audit trail for qualitative conclusions is not a differentiator. It is a requirement. GAQP-compliant implementations meet that requirement by design, without additional engineering.

### For Organizations Adopting GAQP

**Institutional amnesia reversal** — The qualitative intelligence trapped in every document, meeting, and decision ever made by an organization represents an asset that is currently unextracted and irretrievable. GAQP converts institutional memory into institutional infrastructure. The corpus does not forget when people do.

**Decision-cycle compression** — Retrieving relevant precedents, corroborating claims, and prior qualitative analysis across a governed corpus takes seconds, not weeks. The compression changes the pace of organizational decision-making, not just its cost.

**Cross-document synthesis** — Nuggets from a board deck, a vendor contract, a customer complaint cluster, and a regulatory filing can be combined into a single governed synthesis. No document search system produces this. Only a governed atomic layer makes it possible.

## X. The Path to Version 1.0

GAQP `v0.1-working` is the current Schematic. It is structurally complete and operationally deployable. The path to `v1.0` requires:

**Freeze event** — The constitutional content is stable. The freeze sets the release date, computes the package checksum, and locks the version designator. The Schematic becomes the immutable reference for all v1.0-compliant implementations.

**Reference implementation validation** — The first compliant implementation executes against the Schematic and confirms that all 14 .intel file sections, all 29 schema fields, all 7 admission tests, and all 25 claim types function as specified. The reference implementation is the living proof that the Schematic is executable.

**Certification framework** — The L1–L7 conformance ladder is defined. The certification process — how a system demonstrates and documents its conformance level — is the next governance artifact.

**Contribution infrastructure** — The proposal process for statutory-layer additions is defined in `CONTRIBUTING.md`. A governance workflow for processing and publishing accepted proposals at regular cadence is the next operational requirement.

**v1.0 is a freeze, not a finish line.** The statutory layer continues evolving immediately after release. Constitutional improvements accumulate toward v2.0. The Schematic is designed to update continuously without breaking compliant implementations.

## The Ten Core Principles

These principles are not best practices. They are structural rules that any governed qualitative reasoning system must uphold.

1. **Materiality of Judgment** — Distinguish decision-relevant qualitative factors from noise. Not all information is a claim.
2. **Source Traceability** — Every claim must be traceable to an identifiable source, actor, and passage. No floating assertions.
3. **Consistency of Framework Application** — The classification framework applied to claims must remain constant across periods and contexts.
4. **Conservatism** — Weight cautious interpretation. Optimistic readings must be explicitly justified.
5. **Contextual Boundary Disclosure** — Claims must disclose the conditions under which they are valid and the conditions under which they break down.
6. **Multi-Source Corroboration** — Three or more independent sources converging on a claim constitutes a finding. Same-source repetition does not.
7. **Judgment Independence** — The analyst or system producing a claim must be separated from distorting incentives.
8. **Proportionality** — The depth of analysis applied to a claim should be proportional to the stakes of the decision it supports.
9. **Revision Standard** — The conditions under which a claim would require revision must be definable in advance.
10. **Scenario Completeness** — Analysis must account for principal alternative interpretations, not only the preferred one.
